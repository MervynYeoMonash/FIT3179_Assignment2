Oil plays a pivotal role in the global economy. As a primary source of energy, oil fuels economies by powering vehicles, factories, and homes, which causes oil price to have heavy influence on global economy.

The reliance on oil necessitates a careful and strategic approach to ensure energy security and sustainability in the face of evolving global challenges.

Oil price per litre (USD) per Country

The price of oil for each country is primarily influenced by global supply and demand dynamics. Additionally, local factors such as currency strength, taxation policies, and transportation costs contribute to variations in oil prices between countries.

Some countries may deliberately keep oil prices low to support economic growth and alleviate the financial burden on consumers. Countries with abundant domestic oil production such as Venezuela and Malaysia tend to experience lower petrol prices.

Annual Oil Consumption per Capita & Oil Price per Gallon

Countries with high oil consumption does not always translate to higher oil price as high oil production can prevent the oil price from surging.

The oil price also varies for region as geopolitical can affect the oil price. The European countries generally has higher oil price and oil consumption per capita compared to other region as a result of rapid development.

Proportion of Global Oil production by Continent (%)

Asia has the highest proportion of global oil production as it has the largest land size, The Middle East, home to powerhouse oil-producing nations like Saudi Arabia, Iraq and Kuwait, significantly contributes to Asia's prominence in the oil industry.

Top 10 Countries by Oil Reserves (Thousand millions barrels)

Oil reserves are important to a country's economic stability and development. They serve as a vital source of revenue for nations to invest in infrastructure, education, and public services.

Oil reserves also contribute to energy security , reducing dependence on foreign sources and safeguarding against supply disruptions. Countries with substantial reserves often hold strategic positions in global energy markets, influencing diplomatic and trade relations.

Pollution concern over oil consumption

Although there is no clear correlation between oil consumption and CO2 emissions, the consumption of oil is a major contributor to environmental pollution and heightened levels of CO2 emissions .

The burning of fossil fuels , including oil, releases harmful pollutants such as sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter, leading to air quality deterioration and associated health risks.
